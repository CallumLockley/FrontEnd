Law 5: The Referee

Mike Dean cautioning a player for a foul.

Who are they?

A match is controlled by a referee who has the authorirty to enforce the Laws of the Game. Decisions will be made to the best of the referee's ability according to the Laws of the Game and the 'spirit of the game' and will be based on the opinion of the referee who has the discretion to take appropriate action within the framework of the Laws of the Game.

Signals

Compulsory Referee's Equipment

  • Whistle
  • Watch(es)
  • Red & Yellow Cards
  • Notebook - means of keeping a match record

Other Equipment

  • Communication equipment - eg: buzzer/bleep flags
  • Fitness monitoring equipment

Advantage

This can be used in situations where an offence occurs and the non-offending team can benefit form the game continuing such as creating a goal scoring opportunity. Penalising the offence is the anticipated advantage does not ensue at that time or within a few seconds.

Injuries

  • Play continues for slight injuries until the ball is out of play
  • Stops play for serious injuries, ensuring player leaves the field of play. Only rentering once play has restarted

Exceptions to this apply for the following situations:

  • Goalkeeper is injured
  • Goalkeeper and outfield player have collided, needing attention
  • Severe inury has occurred
  • Injuries from physical offence, opponent has been cautioned or sent off - if treatment is quick
  • Penalty kick is awarded and injured player is the taker
